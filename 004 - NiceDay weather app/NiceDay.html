Temperature

The Celsius scale (°C) is used for common temperature measurements in most of the world, were water freezes at 0°C and boils at 100°C at sea-level atmospheric pressure. Some countries like the Fahrenheit scale, on which water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F at sea-level atmospheric pressure.

Rain

If enough moisture and upward motion is present, precipitation falls from convective clouds (those with strong upward vertical motion) such as cumulonimbus (thunder clouds) which can organize into narrow rainbands. The urban heat island effect leads to increased rainfall, both in amounts and intensity, downwind of cities. Global warming is also causing changes in the precipitation pattern globally, including weather conditions across eastern North America and drier conditions in the tropics.
